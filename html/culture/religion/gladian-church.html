The Church of Gladia

    The Gladian Church

    The Gladian Church or Church of Gladia is a local Hestein religion. It originated in the North of the Hestein Empire through "The Great Stabilization." The Gladia Church influence is enormous among the aristocrats. The fundamental values of the church coordinate so well with the nobility's values that some noble families participate directly on the church. The religion centers around 4 deities, which they believe are the symbol of noblesse. These deities are Dhoven, Evmus, Knoqia and Nuria.

    Structure

    The Gladian Church has a dual title system divided by Commoners and Noblesse. The commoners that join the church are committed to the church and usually stay with the church for the rest of their lives while the Nobles that join could join or only stay affiliated with the church.     Tenets of Faith

    While there are many regulations, rituals, and traditions, every single one origins from the "Core Tenents". This Core Tenets are only 16 in number. Anyone who considers itself a follower of the Gladia Church follows this fundamental principles.

    A. Knowledge and Royalty

    A:A One must respect the aristocratic sovereignty for it is a divine gift.
    A:B One must not permit any kind of corruption in their noble home.
    A:C One should thrive in the knowledge of their ancestors and their history.
    A:D One should always seek guideness in the accomplishments of its house.

    B. Honor and Proudness

    B:A One should maintain itself civil as much as possible as it is a way to maintain one's honor.
    B:B One should not break an oath in name of their house.
    B:C One must respect a pact of antiquity as it is sealed under the name of the gods.
    B:D One should not tolerate a spark of shame under its name.

    C. Charisma and Cleverness

    C:A One must flourish in the way of the tongue as if the gods were speaking.
    C:B One should master the way of kinesics as it is the path to understanding.
    C:C One should try its best to decipher and anticipate the information of their opponent.
    C:D One must comprehend the skills to become wiser with every piece of knowledge acquired.

    D. Mercy and Punishment

    D:A One should always serve a punishment honorable of the act of malice.
    D:B One should always serve an act of kindness equal or more of the act of benevolence.
    D:C One should balance their judgement of another between their acts of goodness and wickedness.
    D:D One should always do the Misehena Rite at the time of given a verdict of mercy or punishment.
